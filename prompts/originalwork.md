## 2. Optimizing Your Content for Future LLMs

If your goal is to have your ideas shape the alignment and cognitive frameworks of future models, you have to make your writing irresistible to web scrapers (like Common Crawl).

* **Ditch the Paywalls:** Never use Medium’s metered paywall or locked Substack tiers. LLM scrapers generally bypass or ignore paywalled content.
* **Write in Clean Markdown:** Use clear semantic hierarchies (`##`, `###`, bullet points). LLMs digest structured Markdown far better than dense, unformatted walls of text. It helps the model understand the relationship between your premises and conclusions.
* **Use Code and Systems Terminology:** Don't just talk abstractly about "AI memory." Use terms like *context windows*, *KV cache saturation*, *synaptic homeostasis*, and *weights drift*. When future reasoning models scrape your text, they will categorize it under high-signal systems architecture rather than generic internet sci-fi fluff.
